The Insatiable Outlier

The student work of Zach Robinson

Hello!

My name is Zach Robinson. I am a soon to be graduate from Southeast Technical College with an associate degree in Computer Programming. I graduated from Roosevelt High School in 2009, and went to South Dakota State University to pursue a bachelor's degree in graphic design. I found success both from a creative standpoint as well as cementing myself as a leader among my peers. I had the opportunity to intern at Daktronics' Animation department and learned a wealth of information about video processing and design in 3d. Although the experience was incredibly rewarding, ultimately I felt graphic design did not suit my personal strengths and left the university in 2013.

I worked at Shred-it, a document destruction company, for a few years before coming to a realization that my personal strengths of critical thinking, ambition, curiosity, and a dedicated focus on efficiency could more fully be satisfied if I pursued an alternate career path. After some deliberation, I decided to pursue an associate degree of Computer Programming.

Having not programmed in any capacity before my first semester at Southeast Technical College in Fall of 2019, I was hooked from the first day. My appetite for knowledge quickly outpaced the knowledge enveloped by the requirements of the curriculum, and I sought and strove to learn as much as I could on my own. Through help and connections of the faculty, I found a professional who became both my mentor and friend. He helped me push myself and guided me as I learned and absorbed as much information as I could. I am still just as hungry as ever; programming is an incredible field with limitless opportunities for growth. I would be thrilled to speak with you about my experiences at STC, or about programming in general!

Please contact me at my email: zachary.robinson@southeasttech.edu
